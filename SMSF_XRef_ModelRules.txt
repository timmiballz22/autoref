SMSF_XRef
Formal standard + informal AI execution notes for SMSF audit PDF cross-referencing
Compiled: 2026-04-27
Status: Research and operating standard only. Not a PDF annotation task.

================================================================================
ABSOLUTE SCOPE CONTROL
================================================================================

This file separates two kinds of instructions.

1. FORMAL STANDARD
   Authority-backed rules from Australian law, AUASB standards, ATO/ASIC guidance,
   the uploaded Australian Guide to Legal Citation, and Australian Government
   readability/accessibility guidance.

2. INFORMAL AI EXECUTION NOTES
   Plain operational instructions for the AI model based on observed failure modes:
   missing page/value references, clutter, overlap, filled boxes, bad labels, failure
   to identify fund/FY, and failure to research before applying annotations.

No visual annotation design is represented as law unless an official source actually
requires it. Where a rule is an operational implication, it is labelled as such.

================================================================================
FORMAL STANDARD
================================================================================

F0. FINAL CONFIRMED POSITION

No Australian legislation, ATO guidance, ASIC guidance, AUASB standard, or AGLC
rule found mandates a specific visual PDF annotation style such as red arrows, red
boxes, highlights, tickmarks, added margins, added pages, or internal codes like XR.

The official requirements are about function, not decoration:
- audit documentation must record procedures performed, evidence obtained and
  conclusions reached;
- audit documentation must be understandable to an experienced auditor with no
  prior connection to the audit;
- the specific items or matters tested must be identifiable;
- SMSF auditors must obtain sufficient appropriate evidence;
- SMSF audit work must address material balances, transactions, ownership,
  separation, valuation, contributions, benefits, ECPI/tax and compliance matters;
- citation/cross-reference practice must favour clarity, consistency and pinpointing.

Therefore, the only defensible visual annotation system is one that makes the tested
item clear without obscuring the source record.

F1. AUTHORITY HIERARCHY

Tier 1 - Binding Australian law
- Superannuation Industry (Supervision) Act 1993 (Cth) (SISA).
- Superannuation Industry (Supervision) Regulations 1994 (Cth) (SISR).
- Other legislation only where directly relevant to the tested item, such as
  contributions, tax, ECPI, superannuation guarantee, corporate trustees or
  administrative reporting.

Tier 2 - AUASB auditing and assurance standards
- ASA 200, ASA 210, ASA 220, ASA 230, ASA 250, ASA 300, ASA 315, ASA 320,
  ASA 330, ASA 450, ASA 500, ASA 505, ASA 520, ASA 530, ASA 540, ASA 550,
  ASA 560, ASA 570, ASA 580, ASA 700, ASA 705, ASA 706.
- ASAE 3000 and ASAE 3100 for compliance assurance context.
- GS 009 Auditing Self-Managed Superannuation Funds.

Tier 3 - Australian regulator guidance
- ATO SMSF auditor guidance, including audit documentation, financial audit,
  compliance audit, market value, ownership/separation, contribution evidence,
  pension/ECPI, LRBA, wind-up, ACR and reporting guidance.
- ASIC SMSF auditor registration, ongoing obligations and independence guidance.

Tier 4 - AGLC citation/cross-reference guide
- The uploaded Australian Guide to Legal Citation, Fourth Edition, 2018, is not
  legislation. It is nevertheless relevant for Australian legal citation principles:
  clarity, consistency, pinpoints, cross-references, numerals and currency format.

Tier 5 - Australian Government readability/accessibility guidance
- Australian Government Style Manual: plain language, numbers, currency,
  mathematical relationships, tables and accessibility.
- Australian Government design/accessibility guidance: do not rely on colour alone;
  maintain readability and contrast.

F2. MANDATED / OFFICIAL REQUIREMENTS

F2.1 SISA accounting records and auditability
SISA section 35AE requires SMSF accounting records to correctly record and explain
transactions and financial position, and to be kept in a way that enables accounts,
statements and returns to be conveniently and properly audited.

Formal consequence:
- Source records must remain readable.
- An annotation must not cover or distort the accounting record.
- Filled boxes over values are inconsistent with the purpose of auditability.

F2.2 SISA accounts/statements/annual return/audit
SISA sections 35B, 35C and 35D establish the accounts/statements, audit and annual
return context for SMSFs.

Formal consequence:
- Values in financial statements, annual return labels, ledgers and schedules are
  potential internal tested items.
- External evidence must be traceable back to those values.

F2.3 ASA 230 audit documentation
ASA 230 requires audit documentation to show:
- procedures performed;
- relevant audit evidence obtained;
- conclusions reached;
- significant matters and judgments;
- identifying characteristics of specific items or matters tested;
- enough detail for an experienced auditor with no previous connection to the audit
  to understand the work.

Formal consequence:
- A visible cross-reference must identify the specific tested value and location.
- Labels like "OK", "Matched", "XR", "REF" or "See evidence" are inadequate if
  they do not identify page, value and result.
- Page + amount/value + result is the safest operational format.

F2.4 ASA 500 audit evidence
ASA 500 requires the auditor to design and perform procedures to obtain sufficient
appropriate audit evidence, and to consider relevance and reliability of information
used as evidence.

Formal consequence:
- A cross-reference must not imply support where evidence is partial, inconsistent,
  absent or unreliable.
- Contradictory evidence must be marked as such.
- Absence of support must be stated, not hidden.

F2.5 ATO SMSF financial audit guidance
ATO guidance says SMSF auditors must gather appropriate evidence to support
assertions for material account balances and transactions in signed financial
statements, including market value and arm's-length matters.

Formal consequence:
- Cross-references should prioritise monetary values, account balances, totals,
  subtotals and material transactions.
- Non-value text is secondary unless it proves identity, ownership, FY, source
  reliability or compliance status.

F2.6 ATO documentation guidance
ATO guidance says SMSF auditors must have sufficient documentation to support audit
findings and opinions, including audit plan, evidence, procedures, checklists with
notation, analysis, discussions/correspondence, audit testing/results and reasoning
on significant matters.

Formal consequence:
- Cross-referencing must not be decorative.
- It must show the evidence chain and conclusion.

F2.7 ATO cross-checking guidance
ATO guidance refers to cross-checking bank statement transactions, invoices and
receipts to the trial balance, ledger accounts and financial statements; and checking
source documents against financial statement accounts for classification.

Formal consequence:
- Page/value references must connect internal accounting values to external evidence
  values.
- The core label grammar is:
  "A page [x] $[amount] agrees with B page [y] $[amount]"
  or the corresponding mismatch/partial/no-support form.

F2.8 ATO ownership/separation guidance
ATO guidance requires sufficient appropriate evidence that the fund owns reported
assets and that assets are kept separate from trustee/personal/employer assets.

Formal consequence:
- Asset values and ownership identifiers are high-priority cross-reference targets.
- Fund name, trustee name, account name, title holder and FY may require
  cross-referencing where they prove ownership/source reliability.

F2.9 ATO market value guidance
ATO guidance says SMSF assets must be reported at market value under SISR
regulation 8.02B and that auditors must obtain sufficient appropriate evidence,
using more than one source where required.

Formal consequence:
- Valuation numbers must be linked to valuation evidence by page and value.
- If value support is absent or only indirect, label it as partial/no direct support.

F2.10 AGLC clarity, consistency, pinpoints and cross-references
AGLC states that clarity and consistency are cardinal principles. It defines pinpoint
references as references to a specific page, paragraph, footnote or other section of
a source. It also supports cross-references for clarity.

Formal consequence:
- Use specific page references.
- Do not use unexplained shorthand.
- Use precise, self-contained labels.
- "A page 5 $29,840.00 agrees with B page 6 $29,840.00" is preferable to "XR-01".

F2.11 AGLC and Style Manual numbers/currency
AGLC says numerals should be used for pages, sections, paragraphs and citation
elements, and currency should appear as numerals with a currency symbol or
denominator. The Australian Government Style Manual says currency should use the
symbol followed by numerals, no space, and 2 decimal places where decimals are used.

Formal consequence:
- Use "page 5", not "page five".
- Use "$29,840.00", not "$29840", "$29.84k", or "twenty-nine thousand...".
- Use exact value formatting consistent across both sides of the reference.

F2.12 Style Manual plain language and mathematical relationships
The Australian Government Style Manual says plain language helps users understand,
and mathematical relationships should generally be described in words unless symbols
are needed.

Formal consequence:
- Use "agrees with" and "does not agree with" for non-technical readers.
- Avoid "=", "!=", "XR", "REF", "OK", "TBC" unless specifically requested and defined.

F3. FORMAL LABEL GRAMMAR

F3.1 Match
A page 5 $29,840.00 agrees with B page 6 $29,840.00

F3.2 Mismatch
A page 5 $15,516.80 does not agree with B page 6 $15,077.45

F3.3 Partial support
A page 8 $150,000.00 is partly supported by B page 7 interest evidence only

F3.4 No direct support
A page 8 $150,000.00 has no direct support found in B

F3.5 Multi-page support
A page 7 $41,242.00 agrees with B pages 9-10 pension payments

F3.6 Fund/FY identity confirmation
Fund and FY: A page 1 agrees with B page 1
Use this only if both documents visibly contain the same fund and FY information.

F3.7 Fund/FY mismatch
Fund/FY mismatch: A page 1 [fund/FY] does not agree with B page 1 [fund/FY]

F3.8 Legal pinpoint note where needed
SISR regulation 8.02B - market value support required for this asset value
SISA section 35AE - accounting record must remain audit-readable

F4. FORMAL VISUAL RULES

Status: operational implications, not direct law, unless explicitly stated otherwise.

F4.1 Permitted visible elements when user requests red annotation
- Red text.
- Red arrows.
- Red underlines.
- Red transparent highlights over exact values only.
- Red outline boxes with no fill.
- Red callout labels only where there is whitespace and no overlap.

F4.2 Prohibited visible elements unless expressly requested
- Filled boxes.
- Opaque highlights.
- Added margins.
- Added pages.
- Extra legends.
- Internal codes such as XR.
- Labels without page numbers.
- Labels without values.
- Labels that cover source records.
- Text over source text.
- Arrows that cross unrelated numbers.
- Over-annotation of every number.

F4.3 Box/highlight distinction
- Underline: exact tested value.
- Transparent highlight: exact number/value only.
- No-fill box: exception, unsupported value, mismatch or high-risk value.
- Arrow: points from label to exact value.
- Label: states page + value + result in words.

F4.4 No-fill rule
All boxes must be outline-only. Fill opacity must be zero. If the tool cannot guarantee
zero fill, do not use boxes; use underline + arrow + label only.

F4.5 Text overflow rule
A label is invalid if any text is cut off, clipped, covered, too small to read, or overlaps
source content. Shorten the label or move it to safe whitespace.

F4.6 Non-overlap rule
A label is invalid if it touches, overlaps or visually competes with another label,
original text, a source value, a table border, a signature, a date or a material note.

F4.7 First-page fund/FY rule
On the first page, identify the fund and FY only if the source page visibly provides it.
If the fund/FY is material to source reliability, cross-reference it to the corresponding
page in the other pack. Do not invent or infer fund/FY. If the first page lacks the FY,
look for the FY elsewhere and cite that page. If no FY is found, state "FY not found"
only if this is relevant to the review.

F5. FORMAL PAGE ORGANISATION RULES

F5.1 Page number definition
Use PDF page number unless a different page-numbering convention is expressly requested.
If printed and PDF numbers differ and the difference matters, use both:
A PDF page 5 / printed page 3 $29,840.00 agrees with B PDF page 6 $29,840.00

F5.2 Same relationship on both sides
If A page 5 is linked to B page 6, annotate both pages where practical:
- On A: A page 5 $29,840.00 agrees with B page 6 $29,840.00
- On B: B page 6 $29,840.00 agrees with A page 5 $29,840.00

F5.3 Do not add structure
Do not add index pages, matrices, margins or cover pages unless expressly requested.

F5.4 Do not invent workpaper format
Do not force a generic design. Follow the source pages, the evidence chain and the
user's explicit constraints. A proper SMSF cross-reference is evidence-led.

F6. FORMAL PRIORITY ORDER FOR SMSF CROSS-REFERENCING

Priority 1: Core money values
- Bank balances.
- Bank reconciliation totals.
- Contributions.
- Pension/benefit payments.
- Investment balances.
- Term deposits.
- Managed fund/platform values.
- Property values.
- Rental income.
- Loan/LRBA balances.
- Tax payable/refundable.
- ECPI-related percentages/amounts.
- Variances/mismatches/unsupported amounts.

Priority 2: Evidence reliability / ownership / period identifiers
- Fund name.
- FY/year ended.
- ABN, TFN, ACN/SAN only if relevant.
- Trustee/member names.
- Bank account names and suffixes.
- Statement dates.
- Valuation dates.
- Title holder/ownership documents.
- Auditor/report dates.

Priority 3: Contextual labels
- Section headings.
- Account names.
- Report headings.
- Notes that explain whether a value is gross/net, member-level/fund-level, rounded,
  or total/subtotal.

INFORMAL AI EXECUTION NOTES
================================================================================

This section is for the AI model. It is deliberately direct. It converts the formal
standard and prior failures into a checklist.

I0. NON-NEGOTIABLE OBJECTIVE

Make the PDF easier for an auditor or non-technical reviewer to follow.

Every annotation must answer:
- Which exact number?
- On which page?
- In which pack?
- What is the matching / conflicting / missing evidence?
- On which page?
- What is the result?

If the answer is not visible, the annotation fails.

I1. DO NOT REPEAT THESE FAILURES

1. Do not use XR.
2. Do not use private codes.
3. Do not use labels that say only "matched", "OK", "support", "ref", or "see evidence".
4. Do not omit page numbers.
5. Do not omit the dollar value.
6. Do not focus on general text when the job is about values.
7. Do not create filled boxes that cover the thing being referenced.
8. Do not create text boxes that overflow.
9. Do not create text that overlaps source text.
10. Do not create arrows that point vaguely to a row or section when an exact value is available.
11. Do not add margins unless asked.
12. Do not add pages unless asked.
13. Do not create a new invented style.
14. Do not claim something is law unless it is law.
15. Do not assume the fund name or FY; read them from the document.
16. Do not assume a value is supported if only interest, partial transactions or adjacent evidence is present.
17. Do not over-annotate so heavily that the page becomes harder to read.
18. Do not use colours other than the user-requested colour.
19. Do not use symbols like "!=" for elderly/non-technical readers.
20. Do not skip the final visual QA render.

I2. USER'S SPECIFIC INCORRECT AREAS CONVERTED INTO MODEL RULES

Incorrect area: Referencing should be both pages AND numbers consistently and more if needed.
Model rule:
- Every visible label must include both page references and the exact amount/value.
- Add dates/account names only when needed to disambiguate.

Incorrect area: Focus mainly on the numbers/values of currency to reference and more if needed.
Model rule:
- Start with all dollar values, totals and subtotals.
- Add identity/FY references only when they support the value or source reliability.

Incorrect area: Overflowing text boxes.
Model rule:
- Text box is invalid if clipped.
- Use shorter labels.
- Use two-line labels only if they still fit.
- If a label cannot fit, use underline/arrow and put detail in annotation comment metadata.

Incorrect area: The referencing should always be for numbers and pages and more if needed.
Model rule:
- Page + value is mandatory.
- "More if needed" means date, account name, member name, source type or legal pinpoint.

Incorrect area: Text overlapping and more if needed.
Model rule:
- Use collision checking.
- Labels must not overlap source text, other labels, arrows, values, signatures, dates or table content.

Incorrect area: Didn't research how to label and more if needed.
Model rule:
- Use AGLC clarity/consistency/pinpoint principles.
- Use Australian Government plain-language guidance.
- Use words: "agrees with", "does not agree with", "partly supported by", "no direct support found".

Incorrect area: Didn't research anything and more if needed.
Model rule:
- Before marking a PDF, check the formal standard.
- Identify whether each rule is law, standard, regulator guidance, citation convention, readability guidance, or operational implication.

Incorrect area: Boxes have fill which completely covered the thing and more if needed.
Model rule:
- Boxes must be outline-only.
- Fill opacity must be zero.
- If fill cannot be controlled, use underline and arrow only.

Incorrect area: Speak about the fund and the FY in the first page, and research since it may need referencing elsewhere.
Model rule:
- Inspect page 1 of each pack for fund name, ABN/TFN if visible, FY/year ended, report type and source identity.
- If fund/FY appear elsewhere instead, reference the actual page.
- If the fund/FY differs between packs, mark the mismatch.
- If fund/FY are absent and material, label "FY not found" only after checking relevant pages.

Incorrect area: Box highlighting is wrong.
Model rule:
- Do not use box highlight as a filled rectangle.
- Use red underline for exact values.
- Use transparent highlight only on the exact number.
- Use red no-fill outline only around mismatches or unsupported values.

Incorrect area: Not actually in the cross referencing SMSF format. Fix that.
Model rule:
- SMSF format is evidence-led:
  internal workpaper/account value -> external evidence value -> page/value -> result.
- Prioritise bank, contributions, pensions, investments, TDs, property, loans/LRBA, tax/ECPI and ownership.

Incorrect area: Too hard to understand for auditors.
Model rule:
- Use short, plain labels.
- Avoid codes and symbols.
- Do not make the auditor decode the file.
- The label itself must tell the whole evidence relationship.

Incorrect area: Make them not overlap each other.
Model rule:
- Run visual QA after annotation.
- If labels overlap, reduce label count, shorten wording, move label, or use comments metadata.

Incorrect area: DO DEEPER STANDARD RESEARCH.
Model rule:
- Use official Australian sources first: legislation, AUASB, ATO, ASIC, Style Manual.
- Use AGLC for citation/pinpoint/clarity only.
- Use non-government PDF annotation sources only for tool mechanics, not legal authority.

I3. AI WORKFLOW BEFORE ANNOTATING

Step 1 - Identify document metadata.
- Pack name.
- Fund name.
- FY/year ended.
- Date of report/statement.
- Pages where this metadata appears.
- Do not assume missing metadata.

Step 2 - Build value inventory.
For each relevant number:
- Pack.
- PDF page.
- Amount/value exactly as displayed.
- Nearby label/description.
- Date/period if shown.
- Entity/fund/account/member if shown.
- Value type: bank, contribution, pension, investment, TD, property, tax, ECPI, loan, ownership, other.
- Confidence.

Step 3 - Match values.
For each internal value, find external support:
- Same amount?
- Same date/period?
- Same account/member/fund?
- Same source type?
- Gross or net basis?
- Rounded or exact?
- Material/high risk?

Step 4 - Classify result.
Use one:
- agrees with
- does not agree with
- partly supported by
- no direct support found
- not enough information to conclude

Step 5 - Create labels.
Labels must include:
- Pack A/B.
- Page number.
- Exact value.
- Result phrase.
- Evidence pack/page/value.

Step 6 - Place visual marks.
- Underline exact value.
- Add transparent highlight over exact value only.
- Use no-fill box only if needed.
- Add short arrow to exact value.
- Put label in whitespace.
- Avoid overlap.

Step 7 - QA.
For every annotated page:
- Source value still readable?
- Label readable?
- No overflow?
- No overlap?
- No fill covering evidence?
- Arrow points to exact value?
- Page + value present?
- Result in words?
- Fund/FY checked if relevant?
- Page count unchanged unless user requested added pages?

I4. AI LABEL EXAMPLES

Good:
- A page 5 $29,840.00 agrees with B page 6 $29,840.00
- A page 5 $15,516.80 does not agree with B page 6 $15,077.45
- A page 8 $150,000.00 is partly supported by B page 7 interest evidence only
- A page 8 $150,000.00 has no direct support found in B
- Fund/FY: A page 1 agrees with B page 1
- Fund/FY mismatch: A page 1 FY2024 does not agree with B page 1 FY2023

Bad:
- XR-01
- REF
- OK
- Matched
- See evidence
- A p5 != B p6
- B p6 support
- Supported
- Confirmed

I5. CLUTTER CONTROL

Use the least annotation needed to make the evidence relationship clear.

If a page has many values:
1. Mark totals first.
2. Mark mismatches second.
3. Mark unsupported values third.
4. Mark individual components only if they explain a total or exception.
5. Do not mark every repeated number.

If labels crowd the page:
- Use shorter labels.
- Move labels to nearby blank area.
- Use metadata comments for detail.
- Keep visible label page+value+result.
- Do not use margins unless requested.

I6. FORMATTING CONTROL

Default label font:
- Plain sans-serif.
- Large enough to read at normal zoom.
- Red only if user requested red.
- No bold unless needed for visibility.
- No all-caps except short source labels like A/B.

Default line/box:
- Thin red line.
- Arrow head clear but not oversized.
- Box no fill.
- Highlight transparent and exact-number-only.

I7. FUND AND FY CONTROL

The first pass on any SMSF pack must answer:
- What is the fund name?
- What is the financial year / year ended?
- What page proves it?
- Does the external pack show the same fund/FY?
- If not, where is the mismatch?
- If not found, was it checked across the relevant pages?

Do not annotate values until the fund/FY/source identity risk is understood.

I8. FINAL ACCEPTANCE TEST

The file passes only if:
- Every visible value reference includes page + value.
- Every result is written in words.
- All source values are readable.
- No boxes have fill.
- No text overlaps.
- No labels overflow.
- No unexplained codes appear.
- Fund/FY was checked and referenced where relevant.
- The annotation follows SMSF evidence logic, not arbitrary design.
- The output was visually re-rendered and inspected.

FORMAL LAW EXPANSION - ADDITIONAL AUSTRALIAN LEGISLATION
================================================================================

Status of this section:
- This section adds further Australian legislative hooks without deleting anything
  from the existing standard.
- These provisions do not change the core annotation grammar.
- They strengthen the checklist of what may need page/value cross-referencing in
  an SMSF audit file.
- Use these provisions only when the document contents make them relevant.
- Do not assume a breach, compliance issue, fund status, contribution type, pension
  status, related-party relationship, market value problem, LRBA, or corporate
  trustee issue unless the documents support it.

L0. CORE RULE AFTER ADDING MORE LAW

The annotation rule remains unchanged:

    [Pack] page [PDF page] [exact value] [plain-language result]
    [other Pack] page [PDF page] [exact value/source]

Examples:
    A page 5 $29,840.00 agrees with B page 6 $29,840.00
    A page 8 $150,000.00 has no direct support found in B
    A page 5 $15,516.80 does not agree with B page 6 $15,077.45

More law increases what may be relevant. It does not justify clutter, opaque boxes,
unexplained shorthand, missing page numbers, or missing values.

L1. SUPERANNUATION INDUSTRY (SUPERVISION) ACT 1993 - ADDITIONAL HOOKS
================================================================================

L1.1 SISA section 17A - definition of self managed superannuation fund
Use when:
- The file contains trustee/member structure, corporate trustee/director details,
  member count or SMSF status.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Fund/member/trustee identity may need page references where fund status is relevant.
- Do not annotate identity fields unless they support source reliability, SMSF status,
  ownership, trustee/member authority, or a value.

L1.2 SISA section 17B - trustee/director remuneration carve-out
Use when:
- The file shows trustee or director remuneration or related-party service fees.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Reference the fee amount, page, service basis and any support for arm's-length terms.

L1.3 SISA section 19 - regulated superannuation fund
Use when:
- Compliance status or regulated fund status is in issue.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Fund status documents should be page-referenced if they support the audit conclusion.

L1.4 SISA section 42A - complying fund status for SMSFs
Use when:
- The annual return, audit report or compliance status is in issue.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Compliance status references should identify the document page and FY.

L1.5 SISA section 52B - trustee covenants for SMSFs
Use when:
- Investment strategy, trustee duties, separation, care/diligence, performance review
  or member interests are in issue.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Investment strategy page, trustee minutes, review date and related asset values may
  require page references.

L1.6 SISA section 52C - director covenants for corporate trustees of SMSFs
Use when:
- The SMSF has a corporate trustee and director obligations are relevant.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Corporate trustee/director pages may need references where authority or execution
  supports transactions.

L1.7 SISA section 55 - consequences of contravention of covenant
Use when:
- The audit file identifies potential covenant breaches.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Do not merely label "breach"; reference the exact value/page/event and the source
  support for the conclusion.

L1.8 SISA section 55A - cashing benefits after death
Use when:
- Death benefits, deceased members or death benefit instructions appear.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Reference member/deceased status, benefit value, payment page, and governing-rule
  evidence.

L1.9 SISA section 58 - trustee not to be subject to direction
Use when:
- Member directions, investment directions, external controller notes or related-party
  instructions appear.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Reference the document page showing the direction and any related transaction value.

L1.10 SISA section 59 - exercise of discretion by person other than trustee
Use when:
- Binding/non-binding death benefit nominations, pension discretion, member directions
  or third-party control appears.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Reference the decision document page and relevant benefit/payment value.

L1.11 SISA section 62 - sole purpose test
Use when:
- Private benefit, related-party use, collectable/personal-use asset, property use,
  loan, early access or non-retirement benefit concern appears.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Reference the exact asset/payment/benefit value and the page showing the private
  benefit risk.

L1.12 SISA section 62A - collectables and personal use assets
Use when:
- Artwork, jewellery, coins, wine, vehicles, recreational assets or similar assets appear.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Reference asset value, insurance/storage/lease evidence, and any related-party use
  evidence.

L1.13 SISA section 65 - lending or financial assistance to members/relatives
Use when:
- Loans, advances, member reimbursements, payments to members, related-party debtor
  accounts or financial assistance appear.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Reference the exact dollar amount, member/relative page, bank/payment evidence page
  and classification.

L1.14 SISA section 66 - acquisitions of assets from related parties
Use when:
- Asset purchases from members, relatives, employers, related companies/trusts appear.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Reference purchase price, asset type, vendor relationship, page support and exception
  category if relevant.

L1.15 SISA section 67 - borrowing by regulated superannuation funds
Use when:
- Loans, overdrafts, negative cash, borrowing notes or financing appears.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Reference borrowing amount, lender page, loan statement page and any exception/LRBA
  support.

L1.16 SISA section 67A - limited recourse borrowing arrangements
Use when:
- LRBA, bare trust, custodian trust, property loan, limited recourse loan, holding trust
  or single acquirable asset appears.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Reference asset value, loan balance, holding-trust evidence, lender statement,
  repayment/interest pages and ownership support.

L1.17 SISA section 67B - LRBA replacement assets
Use when:
- Refinancing, substituted security, replacement asset or changed LRBA asset appears.
Cross-reference consequence:
- Reference the original and replacement asset pages and values.

L1.18 SISA section 69 - relatives
Use when:
- Related-party/member-relative analysis appears.

L8. STATE/TERRITORY PROPERTY LAW NOTE
================================================================================

Status:
- Property ownership/title evidence is often governed by State or Territory land titles
  legislation and land registry systems.
- The exact Act varies by jurisdiction.
- Do not cite a State/Territory land title statute unless the property jurisdiction is
  identified in the source document.

Operational rule:
- If the SMSF owns real property, first identify the State/Territory and title evidence.
- Then cite the relevant title search/land registry evidence by page and property value.
- Do not infer ownership from a property address alone.
- Reference title holder, property address, valuation amount, rental amount and loan/LRBA
  amount separately.

L9. UPDATED PRIORITY MATRIX AFTER ADDING MORE LAW
================================================================================

Priority 1 - always assess if present:
- Fund name and FY/year ended on first source page or nearest reliable page.
- Financial statement totals.
- Bank balances and bank transactions.
- Contribution totals and member allocations.
- Pension/benefit payment amounts.
- Investment and portfolio values.
- Term deposit principal and interest.
- Property values, rental income and ownership/title.
- Loan/LRBA balances, interest and repayment amounts.
- Tax/ECPI/NALI/CGT values.
- In-house asset values and related-party values.
- Unsupported or mismatching values.

Priority 2 - assess if tied to a value/compliance point:
- Trustee/director details.
- Corporate trustee ACN/officeholders/execution.
- Member/dependant relationship evidence.
- Condition of release evidence.
- Contribution eligibility/cap evidence.
- Market valuation method/date.
- ATO determinations/release authorities.
- SG/clearing-house contribution evidence.
- Auditor registration/SAN if audit report validity is in issue.

Priority 3 - do not annotate unless needed:
- Logos.
- Generic headings.
- Boilerplate text.
- Generic report paragraphs.
- Contact details.
- Internal codes that do not affect evidence.

L10. UPDATED AI NON-ASSUMPTION RULE
================================================================================

The model must not assume:
- fund identity;
- financial year;
- SMSF status;
- compliance status;
- contribution type;
- pension status;
- condition of release;
- relationship/related-party status;
- asset ownership;
- market value support;
- LRBA status;
- corporate trustee authority;
- GST/tax treatment;
- ECPI entitlement;
- NALI/NALE issue;
- rounding/truncation;
- auditor registration/independence.

The model may only mark what is visible or supported by the documents. If a value is
not supported, the label must say "no direct support found" or "partly supported by",
not "agrees with".

13. FINAL STANDARD TO USE BEFORE ANY PDF ANNOTATION WORK
================================================================================

Before annotation:
1. Build a value inventory.
2. Match each internal value to external evidence.
3. Decide whether each target is material/high-risk/supporting/exception.
4. Prepare labels using page + value + plain-word result.
5. Use only permitted visible markup.
6. Render/inspect the annotated page.
7. Fix all overlap, fill, clutter, page/value omissions, and ambiguity before delivery.

Final rule:

    The annotation is acceptable only if a reader can see the exact number, read the exact page/value reference, understand whether it agrees or does not agree, and still read every part of the original evidence.

================================================================================
YT0. WHAT THE YOUTUBE / TUTORIAL MATERIAL ADDED
================================================================================

The tutorial material confirms the mechanics of professional PDF callouts and markups:

1. A callout is not just a line or a random text box.
   It is a text box connected to an endpoint/leader line that points to a specific
   place in the document.

2. The text box and the leader endpoint can be moved independently.
   Therefore, if the leader crosses text, points vaguely, or makes clutter, it is a
   placement failure.

3. Text boxes must be resized or autosized to fit the text.
   Therefore, overflowing/cut-off text is a direct annotation failure.

4. Fill opacity, line width, text colour, box colour, font size and leader endpoint
   style are configurable in professional PDF tools.
   Therefore, filled boxes covering source values are avoidable and unacceptable.

5. Multiple leader lines are possible, but they should not be used casually.
   Multiple leaders increase clutter and should be avoided unless a single label
   must point to multiple exact values.

6. Highlight, underline, text box, callout, arrow and shape tools are standard PDF
   review tools.
   But standard tools must still be applied with restraint. Their existence does not
   justify clutter, overlap, or obscuring evidence.

7. YouTube/tutorial guidance on annotation reinforces that PDF markups are for
   reviewability and communication. For this task, reviewability means page + value +
   result, not decorative marks.

Tutorial / markup source notes added:
- YouTube: "How to Create Callout in Bluebeam 2026 (Simple Step)" — teaches insert
  callouts, type notes, adjust leader lines, format text, resize/move callouts, and
  fix misplaced arrows or overlapping text.
- YouTube: "How to Add a Callout Note Comment in Your PDF Documents in Foxit PDF
  Editor" — demonstrates callout note comments in Foxit PDF Editor.
- YouTube: "How I Use Adobe Acrobat to Annotate PDFs" — covers Adobe Acrobat
  annotation, font-size adjustment, commenting and highlighting.
- YouTube: "PDF Annotation" — discusses visible annotation/citation inside the text
  rather than forcing separate cross-reference documents.
- Adobe Acrobat help: callouts have a text box, knee line and endpoint line; text
  wraps at the box edge; resize/move the text box and endpoint as needed.
- Bluebeam support: callouts place a leader line connected to a text box; the leader
  terminus and text box can be moved independently; autosize text boxes; move or
  remove leader lines; configure colour, fill opacity, line width, font and margins.
- Foxit annotation material: callouts create a text box and arrow to single out a
  particular area; text boxes are visible on the page and may have optional borders.

YT1. STRICT CALLOUT RULES FROM TUTORIALS
================================================================================

YT1.1 A callout is only valid if all three parts are controlled:
- exact endpoint on the tested value;
- leader line that does not cross unrelated document text/values;
- text box placed in safe whitespace and large enough for its content.

YT1.2 A callout is invalid if:
- its endpoint points vaguely to a row rather than the exact tested value;
- its leader line crosses unrelated numbers;
- its leader line crosses original text;
- its text box overlaps original text;
- its text box overlaps another annotation;
- its text is clipped, overflowed, or too small;
- it needs a legend to be understood;
- it lacks page + value + result.

YT1.3 Use fewer callouts than values when the page is crowded:
- mark only the most important total, mismatch, partial support or unsupported value;
- do not mark every repeated transaction line if a clear total exists;
- do not duplicate labels on the same page if one clear label covers the point.

YT1.4 If a callout cannot be placed safely:
Use only:
- red underline under exact value;
- optional transparent red highlight over exact value only;
- a very short label in nearest whitespace;
- PDF comment metadata for details.

Do not force a callout onto a crowded page.

YT1.5 If there is no clean leader path:
- remove the leader line;
- place the label next to the value if possible;
- use underline + comment metadata;
- do not draw a diagonal line across a table.

YT1.6 If a label is too long:
- shorten it;
- split into two lines only if both lines fit;
- use "A page 5 $29,840.00 agrees with B page 6 $29,840.00";
- do not use paragraphs inside the PDF;
- do not shrink text below readability just to make it fit.

M0. SUMMARY OF PAST MISTAKES THAT MUST NEVER REPEAT
================================================================================

M0.1 Assumption mistakes
- Assumed a made-up cross-reference code "XR" was acceptable.
- Assumed a margin system would help when the user did not request added margins.
- Assumed a matrix/extra page would help when the user did not request added pages.
- Assumed colour-coding systems would help when the user wanted strict conformity.
- Assumed symbols like "!=" were acceptable for pension-age/non-technical readers.
- Assumed identity fields were equally important as values when the task was mainly
  currency/value cross-referencing.
- Assumed partial evidence could be presented too close to full agreement.
- Assumed a visually busy page was acceptable because the content was "technically"
  cross-referenced.
- Assumed OCR/text extraction was enough for a visual PDF task.
- Assumed the file was acceptable without full visual QA at page level.

Strict rule:
Do not assume. Read the page, identify the value, find the supporting page, classify
the relationship, then annotate only if the annotation improves readability.

M0.2 Research mistakes
- Did not research visual workpaper/callout conventions before first annotation pass.
- Did not clearly separate law/standards from operational PDF design.
- Did not treat the uploaded AGLC as citation guidance rather than legislation.
- Did not research enough Australian legal hooks before building the standard.
- Did not research PDF callout mechanics enough before using arrows/callouts.

Strict rule:
Before annotating:
1. Check the formal legal/audit standard.
2. Check the PDF markup rules.
3. Check whether each annotation is mandated, official guidance, or operational
   implication.
4. Never present operational design as law.

M0.3 Label mistakes
- Used "XR" instead of page + value references.
- Used shorthand where plain English was required.
- Used symbols instead of words.
- Used labels that were too long.
- Used labels that lacked either page number or exact value.
- Used labels that did not clearly state agreement, mismatch, partial support or no
  direct support.

Strict rule:
Every visible label must use:
    [Pack] page [number] [value] [result in words] [Pack] page [number] [value]

Approved result words:
- agrees with
- does not agree with
- partly supported by
- has no direct support found in
- not enough information to conclude

Banned visible result codes:
- XR
- REF
- OK
- MATCHED
- TBC
- !=
- =
- tick-only symbols
- colour-only status

M0.4 Visual mistakes
- Lines were too cluttered.
- Arrows overlapped each other.
- Arrows crossed document text.
- Text boxes overlapped document text.
- Text boxes overflowed.
- Boxes had fill that covered evidence.
- Highlights covered too much area.
- Labels were too close to tables and values.
- Margins were bad.
- Styling was inconsistent and not auditor-friendly.
- Annotations made pages harder to read.

Strict rule:
An annotation fails if it makes the page harder to read.

M0.5 SMSF-format mistakes
- Did not consistently follow internal value -> external evidence -> page/value/result.
- Did not focus first on material money values.
- Did not always reference both pages and both values.
- Did not consistently identify fund/FY first.
- Did not clearly separate "agrees" from "partial support" or "no direct support".
- Did not treat valuation/ownership/LRBA/bank/contribution/pension values as risk-led
  priorities.

Strict rule:
SMSF cross-reference format is evidence-chain format:
    Internal workpaper value
    -> external evidence value
    -> page and value on both sides
    -> result in words
    -> no obscuring of evidence

================================================================================
M1. STRICT ANTI-ASSUMPTION RULES
================================================================================

M1.1 Never assume document identity.
Before annotating values, identify:
- fund name;
- trustee name if shown;
- FY/year ended;
- pack name;
- source page proving those facts.

M1.2 Never assume FY from file name.
The FY must appear on a page or be marked "not found" if relevant.

M1.3 Never assume two values match because they are similar.
Compare exact amounts, dates, member names, account suffixes, source type, gross/net
basis and period.

M1.4 Never assume partial support is full support.
If only interest is shown for a term deposit principal, label it:
    partly supported by interest evidence only
or:
    has no direct support found

M1.5 Never assume a transaction belongs to the fund if the page only shows a member name.
Check fund/account identifiers where available.

M1.6 Never assume a bank feed replaces bank statement evidence.
Use the visible external evidence actually present in the pack.

M1.7 Never assume a market value is supported just because a product code matches.
Check value, price date and source page.

M1.8 Never assume one annotation system suits every page.
Crowded pages need fewer labels, shorter labels, and often no leader lines.

M1.9 Never assume more annotations means better cross-referencing.
Over-annotation is a quality failure.

M1.10 Never assume a generated PDF is acceptable.
Always re-render and visually inspect every annotated page.

================================================================================
M2. STRICT PAGE AND VALUE REFERENCE RULES
================================================================================

M2.1 Every visible reference must include:
- source pack;
- source page;
- source exact amount/value;
- result in words;
- evidence pack;
- evidence page;
- evidence exact amount/value if available.

M2.2 Minimum acceptable labels:
- A page 5 $29,840.00 agrees with B page 6 $29,840.00
- A page 5 $15,516.80 does not agree with B page 6 $15,077.45
- A page 8 $150,000.00 partly supported by B page 7 interest evidence only
- A page 8 $150,000.00 has no direct support found in B
- A page 7 $29,400.00 agrees with B pages 6-9 pension payments

M2.3 Labels must use words, not symbolic equality, unless the user explicitly requests
symbols.

M2.4 Page references must use PDF page numbers unless the user requests printed page
numbers.

M2.5 If PDF and printed page numbers differ and both matter:
    A PDF page 5 / printed page 3 $29,840.00 agrees with B PDF page 6 $29,840.00

================================================================================
M3. STRICT VISUAL PLACEMENT RULES
================================================================================

M3.1 Priority visual treatment:
1. Underline exact value.
2. Transparent highlight exact value only.
3. Use a no-fill box only for mismatch, partial support, no support or high-risk item.
4. Use a short label in whitespace.
5. Use a leader line only if it does not cross source text or other annotations.

M3.2 No-fill box rule:
- Fill opacity must be zero.
- If fill cannot be set to zero, do not use a box.
- Never use an opaque rectangle over evidence.

M3.3 Highlight rule:
- Highlight exact numbers only.
- Do not highlight whole tables.
- Do not highlight paragraph blocks.
- Do not highlight rows unless the entire row is the tested item and there is no safer
  alternative.

M3.4 Leader/arrow rule:
- Use the shortest possible leader.
- Do not cross unrelated values.
- Do not cross source text.
- Do not cross another leader line.
- Do not use diagonal leaders through table bodies.
- If no clean path exists, remove the leader.

M3.5 Label rule:
- Label must fit inside its box.
- Label must not overlap document text.
- Label must not overlap other annotations.
- Label must not cover values.
- Label must be readable at normal zoom.
- If label cannot fit, shorten it or move detail to comment metadata.

M3.6 Gutter/whitespace rule:
- Use existing whitespace.
- Do not add margins unless explicitly requested.
- Do not add pages unless explicitly requested.
- Do not place text in margins if it creates bad styling or weak readability.

M3.7 Density rule:
Maximum visible callouts per crowded page should be low.
Prefer:
- one total callout;
- one mismatch callout;
- one unsupported/partial support callout;
not 10+ callouts with crossing arrows.

M3.8 Duplicate-reference rule:
Do not duplicate the same relationship repeatedly on the same page unless it materially
helps understanding.

================================================================================
M4. STRICT QA RULES
================================================================================

Before delivery, every annotated page must pass:

1. Source values remain fully readable.
2. No box fill covers any source content.
3. No text box overflows.
4. No annotation text overlaps original document text.
5. No annotation text overlaps another annotation.
6. No arrows/leader lines cross source values.
7. No arrows/leader lines cross each other.
8. Every label has page + value + result.
9. Fund/FY was checked before value annotation.
10. Page count unchanged unless user requested added pages.
11. Page dimensions unchanged unless user requested resizing.
12. All annotations improve readability rather than merely increasing markup.
13. The file is re-rendered after saving and visually inspected.
14. Any uncertain evidence is labelled uncertain/partial/no direct support, not "agrees".
15. The final response does not overclaim.

If any one of these fails, revise before delivery.

================================================================================
M5. STRICT DELIVERY RULES
================================================================================

M5.1 Do not say "completed" unless:
- the final file was actually created;
- it was visually rendered after creation;
- obvious overlap/clutter was inspected;
- the download link points to the latest corrected file.

M5.2 Do not claim legal compliance.
Say:
- "built against the compiled standard"
- "cross-referenced using page/value evidence logic"
- "visually QA-rendered"
Do not say:
- "legally compliant"
- "cannot be flawed"
- "guaranteed correct"

M5.3 If a source PDF or uploaded standard is inaccessible, say so immediately.
Do not fabricate a result.

M5.4 If the instruction conflicts with the standard:
- obey the user’s explicit visual constraint where safe;
- preserve audit readability and source evidence;
- do not obscure the record.

================================================================================
M6. FINAL AI COMMANDMENT
================================================================================

The model must treat every annotation as a professional audit workpaper mark.

A mark is acceptable only when it:
- identifies the exact tested value;
- identifies the exact page;
- states the result in plain words;
- points to evidence without clutter;
- does not hide or distort the record;
- is understandable without decoding a legend;
- survives visual QA.

If it does not satisfy those conditions, do not place the mark.
